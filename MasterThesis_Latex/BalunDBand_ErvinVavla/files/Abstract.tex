\begin{abstract}

This thesis presents an active balun with low imbalance in D band realized in SG13G3-130nm SiGe BiCMOS technology.

The circuit is designed as a three-stage. The first two stages form the actual balun, while the third one is an amplifier that can be replaced depending on the designer's choice, with the necessary precautions to adapt it to the rest of the circuit.\\
To improve efficiency, the transistor collector currents were set below the optimum value for $f_{\mathrm{T}}$ and $f_{\mathrm{MAX}}$.
High attention was paid to the symmetry of the layout in order to maximize the amplitude balance, while placing the phase imbalance at a second level of importance.

The circuit is supplied by \SI{1.8}{\volt}, the consumed power is estimated to be \SI{53}{\milli\watt} and the simulated amplitude and phase balances achieved are respectively $\pm 0.2$ dB and $-1 \pm 1^{\circ}$ in the \SIrange{100}{200}{\giga\hertz} band. 
The simulated \SI{3}{\decibel} gain bandwidth ranges from \SI{112}{\giga\hertz} to \SI{175}{\giga\hertz}, with a maximum gain of \SI{12.63}{\decibel} at \SI{112}{\giga\hertz} and a minimum gain of \SI{9.63}{\decibel} at \SI{140}{\giga\hertz} and \SI{175}{\giga\hertz}.\\

The following discussions and results are based solely on simulations.\\
The design is created through Advanced Design System (ADS) and Cadence Virtuoso.
\end{abstract}
